environment

  • TODDLER ENVIRONMENT Children from the age of 1.5 – 2.5 years are part of our Toddler Environment

    TODDLER ENVIRONMENT Children from the age of 1.5 – 2.5 years are part of our Toddler Environment. This age is crucial, as children begin to develop their concentration, independence and a sense of order. Opportunities that allow the children to act independently, build coordination of movements and improve concentration. The directress and materials provided play a major role in developing the child’s verbal language. At this stage, we focus on creating a happy, secure environment for the child to begin his education in.

  • PRIMARY ENVIRONMENT Children from the age of 2.5 – 6 years are part of our Toddler Environment

    PRIMARY ENVIRONMENT The Primary Environment encourages self-activity in the areas of Literacy, Numeracy, Sensorial, Cultural and Practical Life. Each activity is carefully structured keeping the universal sensitive periods of learning in mind. This phase in a child’s growth is one during which he/she is most receptive to certain activities. In the Primary environment, children are encouraged to explore and are given freedom within limits. The focus is on real and concrete learning, which lays the foundation for learning abstract concepts at a later stage. Presentations are offered to the children in order to satisfy their natural curiosity. Most importantly, children have complete freedom to repeat activities – as many times as they want – till they are completely satisfied and have mastered the skill. The intentional availability of only one material of each kind in a class of twenty, is to develop the values of tolerance and patience in children, teaching them to share & wait. Through the reinforcement of appropriate behaviour, the directress lays the foundation for an orderly & productive classroom that promotes respect for work and the importance of giving space to others. Choosing the path of Montessori brings riches to the soul and provides the methodology through which we choose to inspire & lead. Not only are we guiding children; we are partnering with families to raise beings who are self-empowered, confident; with a joyful attitude toward lifelong learning.

  • LOWER ELEMENTARY Children from the age of 6 - 9 years are part of our Toddler Environment

    LOWER ELEMENTARY The Lower Elementary environment is equipped with a variety of materials for practice, keeping in mind the psychology and development patterns of growing children. By the time children are nine years old, they: Work in groups, use manipulative materials and by the end of the program, start to develop analytical skills Work on projects of mutual interest with their friends and submit project reports Explore cultures around the world and make sense of the diversity & their place in the universe Become ‘joyfully obedient’ – a state when inner discipline has matured; where he sees the value in the request made by adults and complies willingly Have entered the stage of symbolic thinking and can imagine ideas, objects & concepts without real sensorial experiences Practice inquiry-based learning at their own pace without limits.

  • UPPER ELEMENTARY Children from the age of 9 – 12 years are part of our Toddler Environment

    UPPER ELEMENTARY Children belonging to the Upper Elementary level are nurtured & guided in a way that enables them to develop a deeper concentration of the world around them and the maturity to participate with greater responsibility. Subjects that are part of the Upper Elementary curriculum include Languages (Hindi & English), Math, World History, Geography, Matter & Astronomy, Zoology, Botany and Health Sciences. Apart from these: Work in groups, use manipulative materials and by the end of the program, start to develop analytical skills Public speaking and circle-time activities impart skills that enable them to make wider social connections They are provided with greater stimulus for abstract thought in subjects like cultural geography, spirituality and astronomy At this age, children develop their own sense of morals, thoughtfulness and spiritual questions. Exposure to yoga, cultural study, vipasana, etc. are given to cater to their inner needs. Students are encouraged to become ‘Peacemakers’ We encourage children to internalize ground rules and work better with external authority They develop creativity and originality of thought Children are intrinsically motivated to learn and enjoy academic challenges

About Us

Welcome to SHRI RAM MONTESSORI

Shri Ram Monntessorie House of Children is a leading school in Hyderabad, imparting education based on the globally-acclaimed Montessori method of education Montessori education is an educational approach developed by Italian physician and educator Maria Montessori, and is characterized by an emphasis on independence, freedom within limits, and respect for a child’s natural psychological, physical and social development.We pride ourselves on being one of the few Montessori schools in Hyderabad to subscribe entirely to the Montessori method. Also, we extend Montessori education to Lower Elementary (6-9 years) and Upper  Elementary (9-12 years), and take it forward with the National & International Curriculum for High School, helping students draw the benefits of the Montessori approach till later in their lives.

About Our School

The materials we use in our classrooms are as prescribed by the Montessori method, and of the highest standards – thus, enhancing the learning experience of the child. Our teachers are Montessori-trained and adept at drawing the best out of your child. The Montessori system encourages children to learn at their own pace, picking up diverse skills every new day, without the pressure that usually accompanies learning. As a teacher, this requires special skills and orientation, which all our teachers go through before being given the responsibility of facilitating children in classrooms.

Another key strength of ours is our low teacher:student ratio. Each classroom is facilitated by two teachers, and usually has about 20-24 children. Children, thus, have enough scope for interacting with teachers and getting their queries addressed in detail.

At Shri Ram Monntessorie – just like it is at the best Montessori schools across the world – children are given the freedom to sit and learn at any of the classrooms (across levels). Not just does this respect the natural desire and abilities of each child, it also enables children of different age groups to learn from one another and improve their interpersonal skills. As Montessori children move ahead in their lives, this enables them to feel more comfortable in both large and small groups – crucial in this day and age.

As a true Montessori school, we see several of our students reaping the benefits of the Montessori method with every new day. We are keen to not just educate children, but to shape citizens of the world – individuals who excel in their chosen streams, but also make a positive difference to the society that they are a part of.
